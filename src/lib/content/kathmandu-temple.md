The temple rises above the surrounding rooftops like a snow-dusted dream.

Five tiers of curved eaves stack toward the sky, each one smaller than the last, each dripping with carved dragons and lotus flowers and the serene faces of bodhisattvas. Prayer wheels line the outer walls, spinning lazily in the mountain breeze. The whole structure is painted in faded reds and golds that seem to glow even in the thin winter light.

You climb the worn stone steps. At the top, a pair of massive bronze doors stand open, flanked by guardian lions whose fierce expressions have been softened by centuries of weather and the touch of countless hands.
